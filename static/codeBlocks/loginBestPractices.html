These code blocks protect against software weaknesses such as Improper Restriction of Excessive Authentication Attempts

Two Factor Authentication (2FA)

Using a secondary authentication mechanism such as an RSA ID or mobile token prevents a wide range of login attacks from Spoofing and Brute Force categories .

Account Lockout

The user account should become locked after more than 10 invalid login attempts at a minimum. The event should be logged and the user should be automatically notified by e-mail of invalid activity to their account.

Strong Passwords Requirements

Weak passwords such as admin or 123456 should not be allowed. Passwords should have a good amount of complexity. Password meters should be used to guide users on how to generate a good password during registration. Long passwords that are made up of random words like "BlueBananaSubwayMeter" are more secure than passwords with high character complexity such as "P@ssw0rd" which are easily guessed.

However note that compliance standards such as PCI specifically require a password makeup that includes both letters and numbers.

Password Expiry

Passwords should expire after less than three months to limit the amount of time they could be brute forced through an online brute force or offline cracking.

Never hard-code passwords

A hard-coded password cannot benefit from expiry, reset or lockout.
